You've Landed in My Nest!

Who am I?

My name is Steph Lewis. I'm a 27-year-old descendant of Vikings that still has no idea what I want to be when I grow up. I wanted to work in the medical field when I entered my undergraduate program, and within two years ended up switching majors to Studio Art. I had initially wanted to work in 3-D Animation, but my post-graduate program was discontinued following a series of Art School closures in 2018. Instead, I ended up working through an apprenticeship to become a certified Bench Jeweler. Following a horrible injury to my left hand which happened because of negligent safety policy, I needed to reassess my future. So now, I'm enrolling in Flatiron School's Software Engineering Program with the hopes to land a job as a Visual Artist for Lockheed Martin -- or something akin to that.

Why's Your Name CrestLark?

In the online world, I've elected to go by Lark or CrestLark. I initially decided to use a pseudonym for privacy while I was gaming. Eventually, I also adopted it when writing or posting artwork as well. I first used the name when I was playing Town of Salem almost ten years ago. Now, no matter whether I'm playing Stardew Valley, Dark Souls, or Borderlands, I'll go by the name Lark.

I had mentioned that I was used the name Lark when writing as well. Another "What do I want to be when I grow up" dream of mine was to be an author. There's actually a horrifically cringe-inducing picture of me in my eighth grade year book in which I was voted "Most Likely to be a Best-Selling Author". Interestingly enough, I have actually been rewriting the same novel for almost 15 years, and I'm finally getting close to a completed manuscript. As the novel itself started off as a realistic fiction in which I stupidly promised to write in my classmates as characters, I wanted the most distance I could put between the original short story and my completed manuscript, including distance from my real name.

As for the "crest" part of "CrestLark", I literally chose the word at random because I liked the way that it sounds. It turns out there's an actual type of Lark called a Crested Lark.
